# The Saltworks

## The Boiling Yards

At the low ground of [[cael-morren]], nearest the reach of the sea, stand the Saltworks — a sprawl of pans and furnaces where seawater is boiled down to salt and stranger things. Steam hangs perpetually over its yards; the walls are white-crusted, the air heavy with brine and tallow-smoke. This is dirty, patient, unglamorous work, done at the level the flood will one day claim, and there is a grim pride in the men and women who tend the fires knowing their whole trade must be moved uphill when the water rises. The Saltworks smell of the sea before the sea arrives.

## Where Saltglass Is Made

More than salt is drawn here. The Saltworks are the one place that makes [[saltglass]] — the clouded glass that dims and clears with the coming water, the tide-reader's most trusted instrument. Its making is half craft and half secret: the right salts fired into the melt so that the finished pane answers to moisture no eye can yet detect. A good sheet of saltglass will fog days before the flood a person can feel, and so every prediction sent from the high tower rests, in the end, on what is poured and cooled in these low furnaces.

## The Anchorites' Ground

The Saltworks are also a stronghold of belief. Here [[the-anchorites]] hold their ground — the faction that refuses to accept the drowning as fate, that studies the deep engine hoping to still the tide and make Cael Morren permanent at last. Their leader, [[castellan-brine|Castellan Brine]], keeps council among the salt-pans, close to the low water where the sea's power is nearest and the argument against it most bitter. To the Anchorites the Saltworks are more than an industry; they are a frontier, the place where the city presses hardest against the water — and where, in the uneven turns of the present, the fires burn later into the night than they used to.
