# Oren Quill

## The man with no chart

Oren Quill belongs to [[the-ferrymen|the Ferrymen]], and like all of that trade he lives on the water's changing rather than against it. What distinguishes him is what he carries in his head: the drowned geography of [[the-undercity|the Undercity]], its collapsed arcades and silted stairwells, the lanes that open at low ebb and close again with the flood. Other boatmen keep charts. Oren keeps memory and nerve, and swears the charts are always a season out of date anyway. He can put a skiff through a barnacled gate in the dark by counting the pull of the current against his oar.

## Cargo the tiers forbid

He moves what the tiers would rather not see moved — salvage lifted from resurfaced rooms, letters between people who should not be writing, contraband ferried up from the low floors before the reclamation crews arrive. Much of it passes through the [[reef-market]], the floating bazaar of the mid-tide, where Oren is known, owed, and occasionally hunted. He is not a violent man; he is a slippery one, and in a city of drowning and resurfacing, slipperiness is a survival trait he has refined to an art.

## Listening for the bells

No one who works the Undercity ignores the [[drowning-bells|drowning bells]] — the old submerged carillon that tolls beneath the water at the turns of the tide. Oren reads them the way a Tidewright reads saltglass: a shift in their drowned voice tells him the water is about to move, and he has more than once saved his own life by trusting a bell over a boast. Lately the bells have been ringing at the wrong hours, and Oren, who trusts his ears above all things, does not like it.

## Content with the flux

He wants no part of stilling the tide. A permanent city is a city with nothing to smuggle, no low ebb to plunder, no changing water to hide in — a Ferryman's ruin dressed as salvation. Oren Quill thrives precisely because nothing here holds still, and he will carry cargo, secrets, and the occasional desperate passenger down into the drowned lanes for as long as the sea keeps breathing.

He claims to have run [[the-salt-road]] end to end at full flood, though no chart shows it and no other Ferryman will say the name aloud on the water.
