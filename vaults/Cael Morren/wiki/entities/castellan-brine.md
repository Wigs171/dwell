# Castellan Brine

## The one who would end the drowning

Castellan Brine leads [[the-anchorites|the Anchorites]], the faction that refuses the city's oldest bargain. Where the Tidewrights make peace with the water and the Ferrymen profit by it, Brine sees only a wound reopened every forty years — homes abandoned to the flood, whole lower districts surrendered and reclaimed and surrendered again. To Brine this is not wisdom but a slow, dignified surrender, and the Anchorites' single purpose is to stop it: to still the tide and make Cael Morren, at last, a city that stays.

## Faith in the machine

The Anchorites' hope rests underground, in [[the-deepworks|the Deepworks]] — the ancient half-understood engine that drives the sea's rise and fall. Brine believes what the Tidewrights consider heresy: that the machine which drowns the city can be reached, learned, and mastered, its great slow breathing quieted like a stopped clock. Much of the Anchorites' labour is patient, dangerous descent and study, work that has cost lives and yielded fragments of understanding that Brine guards jealously and interprets with an unshakable, dangerous confidence.

## Ground and craft

The Anchorites keep their ground near [[the-saltworks|the Saltworks]], where the saltglass is made and the smell of brine and hot glass never leaves the air. It is fitting country for them — a place of transformation, of the sea rendered into instrument. From here Brine directs the work below and preaches permanence to a city that has spent generations learning to let go, finding recruits among those who have lost too much to the water to forgive it.

## Prophet or peril

The Quickening has become Brine's proof and Brine's peril at once. To the Castellan, [[the-quickening|the Quickening]] — the tide arriving early and wrong — is either the Deepworks failing, and so a mandate to seize it before it fails catastrophically, or the machine already stirring under someone's hand. Either way it argues for action now. Others fear that Brine, reaching into an engine no one truly understands to still the sea, may be the very hand that wakes it. Castellan Brine does not entertain that fear; certainty, in the drowned dark, is the Anchorites' first article of faith.
