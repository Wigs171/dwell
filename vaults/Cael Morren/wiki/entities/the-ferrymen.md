# The Ferrymen

## Children of the Flux

The Ferrymen are the one faction that would be ruined by dry land. Where the wrights fear the water and the Anchorites would banish it, the Ferrymen love it exactly as it is — restless, rising, never twice the same. Every shifting lane is a livelihood. When a storey floods, a doorway becomes a dock; when it drains, a canal becomes a road. The Ferrymen know both maps and can read the water between them, poling their flat-bottomed boats through a city that is always half-becoming something else.

## Trade Between the Tiers

Their honest work is carriage — grain, tallow, brass, and bodies moved between the tiers as the flood decides which streets are wet. Their real fortune is smuggling. Nowhere is more theirs than [[reef-market|the reef-market]], the floating bazaar of the mid-tide where cargo changes hands over open water and no wright's ledger records the passage. And no one moves cargo they should not like [[oren-quill|Oren Quill]], the smuggler who has memorized the flooded lanes of [[the-undercity|the Undercity]] and can thread the drowned older town in the dark, between its barnacled walls, when the ebb lays them briefly bare.

## No Wish for Still Water

The Ferrymen keep the long view. They measure their fortunes not in a season but across [[the-long-tide|the Long Tide]] itself — the whole slow rise and fall — knowing that a lane worthless this decade will be gold the next. This is why they alone hope the water never stops. A permanent Cael Morren would drown them surer than any flood. So they watch the [[the-quickening|Quickening]] with sharp, ambivalent eyes: an early tide is dangerous, but chaos has always been their weather, and where the wrights see catastrophe coming, the Ferrymen see, as ever, an opening.
