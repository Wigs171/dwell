# The Anchorites

## The Dream of Dry Stone

Where the rest of Cael Morren has made a grim peace with the water, the Anchorites refuse it. They believe the drowning is not fate but a wound — a thing done to the city that can, with enough understanding, be undone. Their creed is permanence: a Cael Morren that stands forever on dry stone, its lower storeys never again given up to the flood, its bells silenced and its ledgers made obsolete. To the wrights who rebuild after each tide, this is madness. To the Anchorites, everyone else is merely drowning slowly and calling it home.

## Students of the Machine

Their heresy has a foundation, and it lies deep. The Anchorites study [[the-deepworks|the Deepworks]], the vast half-understood engine beneath the drowned town that drives the tide like a slow, patient heart. If a machine makes the water rise, they reason, then a machine can be stilled. Generations of them have descended to map its brass and its silence, hunting for the lever that stops the world. Their base sits near [[the-saltworks|the Saltworks]], where the mineral breath of the pans keeps their instruments dry and their tunnels close to the old descents.

## The Castellan and the Danger

They answer to [[castellan-brine|Castellan Brine]], a leader as immovable as the permanence he preaches, who holds that a single act of courage in the dark could end the drowning for all time. His certainty is the Order's strength and its terror both. For now the [[the-quickening|Quickening]] has emboldened them — proof, they say, that the Deepworks is failing and must be mastered before it fails entirely. Others fear the opposite: that hands upon a waking engine will not still it but break it, and that the Anchorites, reaching to save Cael Morren, may be the ones who finally sink it for good.
