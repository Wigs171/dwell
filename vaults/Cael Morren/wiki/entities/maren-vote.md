# Maren Vote

## The apprentice who reads too well

Maren Vote came to the [[tidewright-guild]] as most do, a child handed over in a low-ebb year, salt already in her hair. What set her apart came later, in the quiet hours over the saltglass, when the older apprentices strained to name the hour of the coming water and Maren simply knew it — not by rule but by a feeling in the teeth, a pressure behind the eyes. Her masters called it a gift and watched her the way one watches weather. In a Guild that has made a science of patience, [[reading-the-tide|reading the tide]] by instinct is a talent equally prized and mistrusted.

## First to see the break

She was the one who found it. Bent over the [[the-tide-ledger|Tide-Ledger]] on a night meant for copying, Maren traced a column of readings that should have marched in their old, forty-year lockstep and saw instead that the numbers had begun to slip — arriving early, arriving crooked, refusing the pattern that had held since before her master was born. She had named [[the-quickening|the Quickening]] before anyone would agree there was anything to name. The tide, she said, is coming wrong. The Ledger agrees with her, though few yet want to read it that way.

## Between the gift and the ledger

Her teacher, [[sabel-course|Sabel Course]], loves her and fears for her in equal measure, and their disagreement is the shape of the crisis in miniature: Maren trusts the feeling in her teeth, Sabel trusts the record. Where the Master counsels a century of caution, Maren wants to act on what her body already knows. The Guild elders find her unsettling — a reader who no longer needs the instruments is a reader who might one day contradict them.

## What she carries

Maren keeps a stub of clouded [[saltglass]] on a cord at her throat, a broken instrument no master would sanction, and she reads it anyway. She is young enough to believe the tide can be understood before it drowns them, and honest enough to be frightened by what her own gift keeps telling her. If the Quickening is truly the [[the-deepworks|Deepworks]] waking, Maren Vote may be the first person in Cael Morren to hear it stir.
