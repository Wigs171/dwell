# Beacon Spire

## The Tower Above the Water

Of all Cael Morren, only the Beacon Spire is never drowned. When the flood climbs to the second storeys and the streets turn to green canals, the Spire still stands clear, its lamp burning above the highest reach the sea has ever made. It is the city's one fixed point in a world of yielding stone — a needle of brass-capped rock rising from the tiers of [[cael-morren]], visible from every raft and rooftop. Sailors take their bearings from it. The drowned take their last sight of the sky from it. It is where the city keeps its head above the water, in every sense.

## The Watch of the Tidewrights

The Spire belongs to the [[tidewright-guild|Tidewright Guild]], whose watchers keep its high chamber lit through every hour of the long cycle. From here they practise [[reading-the-tide]] — studying the set of the current, the smell of the wind, the slow clouding of their instruments, to say when the water will come and how high. The reading is never finished; the tide answers to no calendar a person can hold. A watcher may wait a decade for the sign that confirms a prediction made before they were born, and note it down as calmly as the weather.

## Signal and Record

From the Spire's gallery the readings are sent down to the tiers by lamp and by bell, telling each street when to move up, when to reclaim, when to hold. Every signal that leaves the tower is first written into [[the-tide-ledger|the Tide-Ledger]], the master record kept below — every reading of every generation, checked against the last, so that no observation is ever lost to the water. In this the Spire is more than a lighthouse. It is the city's memory as much as its eye, the place where the tide is not only seen but remembered — and where, lately, the newest readings have begun to contradict the old.
