# The Tidewright Guild

## Wardens of the Rhythm

The Tidewright Guild does not fight the Long Tide; it keeps time with it. Where others pray or profit, the Guild measures. Its wrights are surveyors of drowning and resurfacing, trained to know to the season when a lower storey must be abandoned and when it may be scoured clean and lived in again. They hold that Cael Morren is not a city but a tide made habitable, and that survival is a craft of listening — the patient discipline of [[reading-the-tide|reading the tide]] passed hand to hand across generations.

## The Instruments of the Craft

A wright is known by their tools. Chief among them is [[saltglass]], the cloudy pane that greys and clears with the water's approach, worn at the belt and read like a pulse. Every measurement a wright takes is copied, checked, and entered into [[the-tide-ledger|the Tide-Ledger]], the master record from which all predictions are drawn. Above it all stands the [[beacon-spire|Beacon Spire]], the one tower the flood never claims; from its lantern-gallery the Guild signals the turning of the water to every tier below, so that a household kneading bread in the morning knows by dusk whether to carry the loaves upstairs.

## Discipline and Doubt

Authority within the Guild runs slow and cautious, and no one embodies that caution more than [[sabel-course|Sabel Course]], the Master who keeps the Ledger and distrusts anything the old readings cannot explain. The Guild's virtue is its conservatism; its danger is the same. For centuries the readings held, and the wrights grew proud of their certainty. Now that certainty is cracking. Tides arrive early. Columns that agreed for lifetimes disagree. The younger wrights whisper of the [[the-quickening|Quickening]], while their elders reach, again and again, for the Ledger — as if the answer to a breaking pattern could still be found among the patterns already written down.
