# The Undercity

## The Town Beneath

Beneath [[cael-morren]] lies the town it used to be. Most of the years it is a country of water — flooded lanes, roofless halls, streets a diver would drown in. But at the low ebb of the tide the sea withdraws far enough that the Undercity climbs back into the air, dripping and barnacled, wearing its long submersion like green fur. For one season it can be walked. Salt runs from every lintel; kelp hangs where banners hung; the mud gives up old coins and older bones. Then the water comes again and takes it, patient as always, back into the dark.

## What the First Sinking Left

The Undercity is the wound of [[the-first-sinking]], the founding catastrophe that pulled the old town under when [[the-deepworks]] — the ancient engine below — first drew breath. Whole quarters went down in a single tide and never fully rose. Now, when the sea turns at the great cusps of the cycle, the [[drowning-bells]] ring from the drowned steeples, sounding underwater, a slow submerged tolling felt in the chest before it is heard. To walk the low-ebb lanes to that sound is to walk a graveyard that refuses to stay buried.

## Voices in the Drowned Water

At the heart of the ruin stands a bell-tower the sea never wholly leaves, and there lives [[the-drowned-oracle]] — a woman who breathes the water and speaks the tide's prophecy in a voice thick with brine. Few reach her without a guide, and the surest guide is [[oren-quill]], a Ferryman who knows the flooded lanes by memory and rope, who can bring a boat through streets no chart admits. Deeper still, past the reach of any lamp, waits [[the-deepworks|the Deepworks]] itself, the half-understood machine that drives the whole slow drowning of the world. The Undercity is the door to it — and lately, in the uneven turns of the water, that door has begun to open at the wrong hours.

Below even the drowned lanes, divers whisper of [[the-third-city]] — older than the Undercity, glimpsed once a generation when the ebb runs deeper than the ledgers say it can.
