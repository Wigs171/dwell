# The Long Tide

## A Lifetime in Its Breath

The sea that surrounds [[cael-morren|Cael Morren]] does not answer to the moon in the way of common water. It rises and falls across a slow arc of roughly forty years, one full breath spanning nearly a human life. A child born at the ebb may see the streets they played in first drown, then surface again, wrinkled and grey, in time to teach their own grandchildren to read the same barnacled stones. The Long Tide is not weather. It is the calendar of the world, and every birth, marriage, and burial is timed against it.

## Drowning and Resurfacing

At full flood the water climbs to the second storeys, and the people move up. Cael Morren is built for this: its lower floors are meant to be given to the sea and reclaimed later, tier stacked upon tier, so that the city is never truly lost, only lent. At the deep ebb the old drowned town — the [[the-undercity|Undercity]] — lifts dripping into the air, walkable for a single precious season before the water takes it back. To live here is to hold nothing as permanent and to grieve nothing as gone. Everything sinks; everything is rebuilt.

## The Engine Beneath

The tide is not natural, and the city has never pretended otherwise. Far below the Undercity turns the [[the-deepworks|Deepworks]], an ancient machine older than memory, working the great cycle like a slow patient engine. When it first hauled the old town under, that was the [[the-first-sinking|First Sinking]], the wound from which all the world's rhythm now flows. Whether the Deepworks was built to drown or built to spare, no living hand knows.

## Keeping Faith With the Water

Because a mistimed reading can drown a household in their beds, an entire craft has grown up around foreseeing the tide's turns — [[reading-the-tide|reading the tide]], the patient art of clouding glass and counting seasons. For centuries the readings held true, and the city set its whole life by them. Now they are failing. The [[the-quickening|Quickening]] has come, and the tide arrives early and uneven, breaking marks that stood for generations. What was once as sure as sunrise has become a question, and Cael Morren waits, listening, for the water it can no longer trust.
