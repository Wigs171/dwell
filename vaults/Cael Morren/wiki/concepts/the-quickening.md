# The Quickening

## The Tide Arrives Early

For centuries the sea kept its word. The forty-year breath of the [[the-long-tide|Long Tide]] came and went by marks a reader could set a life by, and the city rose and drowned and rose again on schedule, tier upon tier. No longer. The tide has begun to arrive *early*, and worse, *unevenly* — flooding one quarter while another still stands dry, turning at hours no glass foretold. The Quickening is the name the city has given to this breaking of its oldest certainty, and it is the great dread of the present age.

## First Seen in the Ledgers

It was no seer who first named it but an apprentice's careful eye. [[maren-vote|Maren Vote]], reading back through the master record, found the readings drifting — the marks arriving sooner each turn, a creeping error too regular to be mere mistake. She carried the figures to her teacher, and the conservative master doubted them, as a keeper of centuries must. But the drift was real, and the ledgers do not lie. The Quickening had been written in the record for years before anyone had the courage to read it plainly.

## The Engine at the Heart of It

Whatever is breaking the tide is breaking it from below, in the [[the-deepworks|Deepworks]] — the ancient machine whose slow turning is the tide itself. Its beat has changed, and no one agrees why. Some hold that the great engine is failing at last, worn out by ages of labour; others fear it is waking to some purpose its long quiet only masked. Between failure and waking the whole meaning of the crisis hangs, and the deep offers no answer, only the fact of the change.

## The City Divided

The Quickening has set the factions against one another as never before. The [[the-anchorites|Anchorites]] read it as proof they were always right — that the drowning must be stopped now, the Works stilled before it destroys them all. The [[tidewright-guild|Tidewright Guild]], whose whole authority rests on readings that no longer hold, is thrown into quiet crisis, its masters clinging to a craft the tide has begun to mock. And through the widening cracks the smugglers thrive, for chaos is a currency. Nothing is settled. The tide, once the one sure thing in the world, has become the question every hand in Cael Morren is now forced to answer.
