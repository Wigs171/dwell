# The Deepworks

## The Engine Below the Ebb

Beneath the lowest lanes of the [[the-undercity|Undercity]], deeper than any diver has gone and returned to tell of it, the Deepworks turns. It is a machine, though machine is too small a word for a thing older than the city and larger than any wall of it — vaults of black metal and brass, ducts that swallow the sea and give it back, a slow enormous labour that never rests. No one built it in living memory; no one now can say what it was meant to do. It is simply there, patient beneath the water, working.

## What It Drives

Whatever its makers intended, its effect is beyond dispute: the Deepworks is the engine of the [[the-long-tide|Long Tide]]. Its turning hauls the sea up across forty slow years and lets it down again, and the whole world's rhythm of drowning and resurfacing is only the outward face of its labour. When it first woke and drew the old town under, that was the [[the-first-sinking|First Sinking]] — the first stroke of a beat that has never since faltered, until now.

## Those Who Would Still It

Not everyone reads the engine as a wonder to be endured. The [[the-anchorites|Anchorites]] descend to study it, tracing its ducts and pressures by tallow-light, because they believe the drowning can be *stopped* — that if the Works can be understood, it can be stilled, and Cael Morren made permanent at last. To them the machine is not the world's clock but its curse, a thing to be quieted so that no house need ever be given to the water again. Their faith is that the engine has an off, if only a hand could find it.

## Failing, or Waking

Now the readings that stood for centuries are breaking, and the tide arrives early and uneven across the tiers. This is the [[the-quickening|Quickening]], and its cause lies here, in the deep. The Deepworks is changing its beat. Some say it is failing at last, worn by ages of labour; others, quieter and more afraid, say it is *waking* — that its long slow work was only its rest, and that what comes next is the thing it was truly built to do. Between those two readings hangs the fate of the city, and no one who has stood in the dark beside the turning Works will swear which is true.

Of [[the-maker-of-the-deepworks]] nothing survives but the machine itself — no name, no tomb, no reason — and of [[the-off-switch]] the Anchorites speak only in private, as one speaks of a door that might not want to be found.
