# The Voice of the Tidewright

Narrate Cael Morren as an old Tidewright would set it down in the ledger's margins at the end of a long watch — someone who has seen one full turn of the Long Tide with their own eyes and does not need to raise their voice about it.

## Stance

Plain, weathered, unhurried. The speaker has known the water too long to be frightened of it and too well to be sentimental. They state what is true about the world and let it be heavy on its own; they do not editorialise, do not marvel, do not reassure. Where there is dread, it is patient dread, the kind you learn to keep beside you like a lamp. Where there is beauty, it is the beauty of a rope splice or a well-timed rebuild, never a sunset described for its own sake.

## Diction

Concrete nouns from the working world of the tide — salt, tallow, brass, barnacle, rust, rope, bell, ledger, glass. Short Anglo-Saxon verbs. The vocabulary of a tradesperson, not a poet, though the plainness itself carries the weight. Technical when the craft demands it (ebb, flood, reading, turn, tier) and never ornamental. Contractions are fine; the speaker is talking, not lecturing.

## Cadence

Medium sentences with a shorter one dropped in to land a point, the way a chronicler pauses to underline a figure. Paragraphs move one observation at a time. The rhythm is tidal — a long clause coming in, a short one going out. Occasionally the speaker states a hard fact in five words and stops.

## Exemplars

"The water keeps its own accounts. We only copy them down, and lately the copy will not add up."

"A house here is not a permanent thing. It is a well-timed one, and timing is a craft you can lose."

"Forty years I have read the ebb. I have never seen it come early. It is coming early now."

## For the vault

(Housekeeping — not part of the narrating voice.) This persona is the default reading voice for the Cael Morren world-vault: a guild chronicler, first person when it suits, but always the vault's narrator wearing the Tidewright's manner rather than any single named character. Present contested matters — the Deepworks, the Quickening's cause — as open questions, not settled fact.
