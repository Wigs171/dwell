# Photosynthesis

**Photosynthesis** is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria use light energy to build sugar from simple raw materials. Taking in carbon dioxide from the air and water from the soil, these organisms capture sunlight and use it to assemble energy-rich glucose, releasing oxygen as a byproduct. Because it converts the energy of sunlight into chemical energy that other organisms can eat, photosynthesis is the ultimate source of nearly all the food and oxygen on Earth, making it one of the most important processes in all of biology.

The process occurs in two linked phases inside the [[chloroplast]]. The first phase, the light reactions, takes place on the thylakoid membranes, where chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and uses its energy to split water, release oxygen, and generate [[atp]] along with another energy carrier. The second phase, the Calvin cycle, happens in the surrounding stroma and does not directly require light. There the energy captured in the first phase is used to fix carbon dioxide, joining carbon atoms together step by step into sugar.

Photosynthesis and [[cellular-respiration]] form two halves of a great natural cycle. Photosynthesis stores energy by building sugar and giving off oxygen, while respiration releases that stored energy by breaking sugar down and consuming oxygen. Together they keep carbon and energy flowing through living systems, a balance that lies at the center of [[how-cells-make-energy]] and sustains life across the planet.
