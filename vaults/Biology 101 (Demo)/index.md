## Concepts

- [[ATP]] — ATP is the energy-carrying molecule that powers most cellular work by releasing energy when its phosphate bonds are broken.
- [[Cell Membrane]] — The cell membrane is a selectively permeable phospholipid bilayer studded with proteins that surrounds every cell and controls what passes in and out.
- [[Cell Nucleus]] — The cell nucleus is the membrane-bound control center of a eukaryotic cell that houses the chromosomes and is the site where DNA is transcribed into RNA.
- [[Cell Theory]] — Cell theory states that all organisms are made of cells, that the cell is the basic unit of life, and that all cells arise from pre-existing cells.
- [[Cellular Respiration]] — Cellular respiration breaks down glucose using oxygen to produce ATP through glycolysis, the citric acid cycle, and the electron transport chain.
- [[Chloroplast]] — The chloroplast is the organelle in plants and algae that captures light energy and carries out photosynthesis.
- [[Chromosome]] — A chromosome is a long DNA molecule wound around histone proteins into a compact structure, with humans carrying 46 in most cells.
- [[Cytoskeleton]] — The cytoskeleton is a network of protein filaments — microfilaments, intermediate filaments, and microtubules — that gives a cell shape, support, and the ability to move and divide.
- [[Diffusion and Osmosis]] — Diffusion is the passive movement of molecules down a concentration gradient, and osmosis is the diffusion of water across a selectively permeable membrane.
- [[DNA]] — DNA is the double-helical molecule that stores hereditary information using a four-letter chemical alphabet read in complementary base pairs.
- [[DNA Replication]] — DNA replication is the semiconservative copying of DNA before cell division, carried out by helicase and DNA polymerase along leading and lagging strands.
- [[Ecosystem]] — An ecosystem is a community of interacting organisms together with the physical environment they share, linked by energy flow and nutrient cycling.
- [[Endomembrane System]] — The endomembrane system is a connected set of organelles — the endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, vesicles, and lysosomes — that makes, modifies, packages, and transports proteins and lipids.
- [[Enzyme]] — An enzyme is a biological catalyst, usually a protein, that speeds up chemical reactions by lowering their activation energy.
- [[Eukaryotic Cell]] — A eukaryotic cell has a true membrane-bound nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles, and is the cell type found in animals, plants, fungi, and protists.
- [[Evolution]] — Evolution is the change in the heritable traits of populations across successive generations, producing descent with modification from common ancestors.
- [[Gene]] — A gene is a segment of DNA that codes for a functional product, usually a protein, and serves as the basic unit of heredity.
- [[Glycolysis]] — Glycolysis is the cytoplasmic pathway that splits glucose into two pyruvate molecules, producing a small net yield of ATP.
- [[Homeostasis]] — Homeostasis is the maintenance of stable internal conditions in an organism through self-regulating feedback mechanisms.
- [[Meiosis]] — Meiosis is a two-stage cell division that produces four genetically varied haploid gametes from one diploid cell.
- [[Mitochondrion]] — The mitochondrion is the membrane-bound organelle where eukaryotic cells carry out most aerobic respiration to produce ATP.
- [[Mitosis]] — Mitosis is the division of a cell's nucleus that produces two genetically identical daughter cells for growth and repair.
- [[Natural Selection]] — Natural selection is the process by which heritable traits that improve survival and reproduction become more common in a population over generations.
- [[Photosynthesis]] — Photosynthesis uses light energy to convert carbon dioxide and water into sugar and oxygen through the light reactions and the Calvin cycle.
- [[Prokaryotic Cell]] — A prokaryotic cell is a small cell that lacks a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles, with its DNA in a nucleoid region; bacteria and archaea are prokaryotes.
- [[Protein]] — Proteins are chains of amino acids folded into specific shapes that perform most of the work of the cell, from catalysis to structure to signaling.
- [[Ribosome]] — The ribosome is the molecular machine that reads messenger RNA and assembles amino acids into proteins.
- [[RNA]] — RNA is a single-stranded nucleic acid that uses uracil and serves as messenger, transfer, and ribosomal molecules in gene expression.
- [[Transcription]] — Transcription is the synthesis of messenger RNA from a DNA template by the enzyme RNA polymerase, the first step in expressing a gene.
- [[Translation]] — Translation is the process in which ribosomes read mRNA codons with the help of tRNA to assemble amino acids into a polypeptide.

## Entities

- [[Anton van Leeuwenhoek]] — Dutch microscopist who built powerful single-lens microscopes and became the first person to observe living microorganisms, which he called "animalcules."
- [[Carl Linnaeus]] — Swedish naturalist who founded the modern system of biological classification and introduced the two-part naming system known as binomial nomenclature.
- [[Charles Darwin]] — English naturalist who proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection and published On the Origin of Species in 1859.
- [[Gregor Mendel]] — Austrian friar and scientist whose experiments with pea plants revealed the basic laws of inheritance and founded the science of genetics.
- [[Lynn Margulis]] — American biologist who developed and championed the endosymbiotic theory explaining the origin of mitochondria and chloroplasts from once free-living bacteria.
- [[Robert Hooke]] — English scientist whose 1665 book Micrographia introduced the term "cell" after he observed the structure of cork under a microscope.
- [[Rosalind Franklin]] — British chemist whose X-ray diffraction images, including the famous "Photo 51," were critical to determining the double-helix structure of DNA.

## Syntheses

- [[From DNA to Protein]] — The central dogma describes how genetic information flows from DNA through RNA to build proteins by way of transcription and translation.
- [[How Cells Divide]] — Mitosis produces identical cells for growth and repair while meiosis produces varied gametes, both beginning after DNA replication copies the chromosomes.
- [[How Cells Make Energy]] — Photosynthesis stores light energy in sugar while cellular respiration releases that energy as ATP, forming a continuous biological energy cycle.
- [[How Life Is Organized]] — Living systems are organized in a nested hierarchy from molecules through cells and organisms up to ecosystems, with species named by Linnaean classification.
- [[Prokaryotes vs. Eukaryotes]] — Prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells differ in their nucleus, internal compartments, and size, with eukaryotic organelles tracing to an endosymbiotic origin.
- [[The Voice of the Naturalist]] — The default narrator persona for the Biology 101 vault — a warm, curious field-naturalist voice.
- [[Why Evolution Unifies Biology]] — Evolution by natural selection explains the shared molecular machinery and deep relatedness of all living things, making it biology's central unifying theory.
