# Gene

A **gene** is a segment of [[dna]] that contains the instructions for making a functional product, most often a [[protein]]. In a sense, a gene is a single meaningful passage within the much longer text of the genome. The order of bases along that segment spells out, in a chemical code, the sequence of building blocks needed to construct its product, so each gene determines a specific molecule that contributes to the structure or activity of the cell.

The gene is also the basic unit of heredity, the thing that is passed from parent to offspring. Long before anyone knew DNA existed, [[gregor-mendel]] inferred from his experiments with pea plants that inherited traits are governed by discrete factors that come in pairs and are passed on intact from one generation to the next. Those abstract factors are what we now call genes. Many traits arise from different versions of a gene, called alleles, which can produce visible differences such as flower color or seed shape.

Genes do not float freely; they are arranged in a definite order along each [[chromosome]], and the position a gene occupies is called its locus. When a cell needs a gene's product, it does not use the DNA directly. Instead it copies the gene into a working message and then reads that message to build the protein. Through this orderly flow of information, genes link the stable archive of heredity to the everyday chemistry of life, ensuring that the traits of one generation appear again in the next.
