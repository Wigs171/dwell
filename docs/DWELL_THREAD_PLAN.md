## T1/T6 — THE UNDERCURRENT (tween connectivity), verified design

**Reframe:** tweens don't lack causality, they lack *membership*. A human lull
belongs to something that recurs (a person, a question, a slow change); an
inserted page belongs to nothing. Subplot presence and braided strands are
measured HUMAN signatures — so the fix aligns connectivity with humanlikeness
instead of against it (via's failure).

**What the verifiers KILLED (design-shaping refutations):**
1. *"Prompt-side data is pink-elephant-safe"* — FALSE. Via's line was
   prompt-side and still collapsed P(human). The damage is what the data does
   to the prose's SHAPE, not word leakage → every strand line must pass the
   P(human) hard-gate; subtler is safer.
2. *Corridor replanning on steer* — doesn't exist. The spine is FIRM; steering
   = drift via propose() producing eventless 'move' pages, and drift resets
   `_tween_k`. Reader-chosen lulls are NOT bridge-mode — a bridge-only
   mechanism misses the live app's actual lulls.
3. *Planner-authored beat ladders* (scheduled undercurrent beats) — a
   pre-authored second chain: chore-risk, chain-risk. Killed.
4. *Rolling strand memory in the cache key* — race-dependent (server builds
   the next plan before the digest thread lands); repeats the hand-ordinal
   bug class. Killed.
5. *New commit-time ledgers* must be re-copied in `peek_after` (copy.copy
   shares them; the prefetch path pollutes) — a precondition, not a nicety.
6. Good news refutation: `observe_canon` ALREADY harvests waypoint figures —
   re-met figures are identity-held by the p26 ledger for free.

**What survived (the lean build, per-component arms):**
- **T6a — thread-on-quiet-pages:** extend the T5 hold to *eventless* pages
  (bridges AND reader-drift moves — the custody rule stated structurally:
  the undercurrent lives wherever there is no plot_event; gates never see it).
  A carried vow turned over in a lull is idling, not work. ~Two lines.
- **T6b — re-meet corridors:** an encounter ledger of figures/waypoints from
  already-committed pages; every ~2nd–3rd corridor prefers re-meeting one over
  fresh scenery (rarity-capped against "of all people, Bram again!").
  Recurrence EMERGES from the walk's own history — no planner authorship, no
  second chain to leak, cannot ghost (canon holds identities), cannot
  constrain steering (built post-hoc from wherever the reader actually went).
  This is the canonical human lull: running into someone you've met.
- **T6c — steering-precedence deferral** (from Design 2, the one piece that
  survived): a fresh steer suppresses any strand touch that page — deferred,
  not dropped. The reader's channel outranks the strand, honestly ragged.
- **Rest-licensing + monotonicity scan:** the strand may rest; a referee scan
  for the dutiful +1-per-page gradient (the second-order machine tell).
- **T6d — planner UNDERCURRENT card: DEFERRED** until T6a/b verdicts. If
  emergent membership moves `connected` without P(human) cost, authored
  strands may never be needed (and tween hold-budget is already crowded:
  steer + waypoint + mood + hand + canon).

**Probe design:** T6a and T6b as separate same-seed arms; instruments = judge
`connected` (primary), subplot-INTEGRATION (contrasting/ironic vs parallel —
presence alone can read grafted), TMP_ORD_007 braid, P(human) hard-gate with
via as the named falsifier, lull-referee question ("rest/texture or plot
chore?"). Steering-stress arm requires gen_path steer plumbing first
(unbudgeted; noted).
